— a quiet space, made on purpose

about StillHere.

we built this for the moments when everything feels heavy and you don't know where to put it. not a clinic, not a forum — a small, soft place on the internet where presence matters more than answers.

— our one-line
presence, not solutions.

you don't always need to be fixed. sometimes you just need to be heard — and to know someone, somewhere, gets it.

— 01

why this exists

the short version

most of the internet is loud. it wants your attention, your reaction, your hot take. mental-health spaces aren't always better — they push productivity, positivity, five-step plans.

sometimes none of that fits. sometimes you just need somewhere slower. somewhere a stranger can read what you wrote and not try to sell you anything, fix you, or rush you toward "better."

so we made one. it's small on purpose. it's anonymous on purpose. and it stays free, because the people who need it most can rarely afford one more subscription.

— the origin

it started in a comment section

not a startup idea
someone else's post — comments
a video about pretending you're fine when you're not.

@someone_quiet

everyone around me thinks i'm doing fine and i'm too tired to explain that i'm not.

@quiet_reader

i read every word.
you don't have to be the strong one here.
i'm sitting with you for a minute. that's all.

people kept writing honest things in places never meant for them — comment sections, random posts, late-night threads. not because they wanted attention. because they needed somewhere to put what they couldn't say out loud.

(i noticed it most on TikTok, but it's everywhere.)

the pattern was always the same: someone writing the most honest thing they'd said in months, sideways, under content that had nothing to do with them — and hoping a single stranger would notice.

sometimes one did. and the only thing they said back was something like "i read every word." — and that was the only place all week the person who wrote it felt heard. someone actually stopped and listened.

StillHere is that — on purpose. not buried under a video your friends might see. not borrowed from someone else's thread. a quiet feed where you can write what you couldn't say out loud, and someone who's been there too can sit with you for a minute.

you shouldn't have to look strong
to be allowed to say something true.

— 04

what we're not

honest about the edges

we're not a replacement for —

StillHere is a soft place, but it has limits. please know them, and please don't carry serious things alone.

a therapist or psychiatrist. we can't diagnose, prescribe, or treat clinical conditions.
a crisis line. if you're unsafe right now, please call a trained counselor — they're 24/7 and free.
a debate forum. unkindness and "well actually" replies get removed.
a place for promotion. no products, no programs, no "DM me my e-book."
— 05

who's behind this

just one person
— the person

one person, working quietly.

StillHere is a solo project. one developer, building and maintaining the whole thing — the site, the database, the moderation, the writing on these pages, all of it. no team, no investors, no growth deck.

the reason it exists is personal. burnout, grief, the kind of nights where the internet only made it worse. nothing felt right — so this place is an attempt to make one that doesn't shout.

everything is open-source. no ads, no data sales, no quarterly numbers to hit. running costs come out of one person's pocket; if it ever grows, it'll grow slowly and only as much as it needs to.

if you want to help — share your story, send a kind reply, point out a bug, or just exist here quietly — you already are.

— 06

inspirations & honest credit

we didn't invent quiet

StillHere stands on the shoulders of projects that figured out something tender long before we did. quiet letters, our wall of unsent messages, is openly inspired by The Unsent Project by Rora Blue — a years-running collection of messages to first loves, sorted by colour. it's a quiet, generous piece of internet, and the format clicked into what we were already building.

we kept what mattered — no replies, just colour and short honesty — and shaped it toward this place's purpose: not only first loves, but the parent who can't hear it anymore, the version of you that didn't make it through, the dog you forgot to thank. if you've never been there, go. it's worth your evening.

wherever you are tonight — we're glad you found this.

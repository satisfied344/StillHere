Community Guidelines

StillHere exists so people can be heard – not fixed, not judged, not advised unless they ask. These guidelines protect that space.

Effective date: April 13, 2026 Last updated: April 13, 2026 Applies to: all users
The spirit of StillHere: This is a space for presence, not solutions. People come here to feel less alone – not to be saved, diagnosed, or advised. Everything in these guidelines flows from that one idea.
1

Be Kind & Respectful

Every person who posts here is showing a kind of courage. They chose to say something, often for the first time. Treat that with care.

Kindness doesn't mean agreement. You can disagree, you can feel differently – but the way you express it matters.

Do this
  • Acknowledge what someone shared before responding
  • Use "I" statements when sharing your perspective
  • Give people space to feel what they feel
  • Be patient with people who are struggling to articulate
  • Remember there's a real person behind every post
Avoid this
  • Dismissing or minimising someone's experience
  • Comparing pain ("others have it worse")
  • Tone-policing or telling people how to feel
  • Sarcasm or irony that could be read as mockery
  • Arguing about whether someone's feelings are valid
2

Respect "No Advice" Tags

The No Advice tag is one of the most important features on StillHere. When someone tags their post with it, they are explicitly telling you what kind of support they need – which is presence, not solutions.

Respecting this tag is not optional. Posting advice on a No Advice post – even if it's well-meaning, even if you genuinely think it would help – overrides the author's stated wishes. That is a violation of their trust and of these guidelines.

What counts as advice?

Advice includes suggestions, recommendations, solutions, therapeutic techniques, book references, medication mentions, "have you tried…", "you should…", "maybe consider…" – anything that implies the person needs to do something differently.

What's still okay on a No Advice post?

  • Saying "I hear you" or "thank you for sharing this"
  • Sharing that you've felt similarly, without pivoting to how you fixed it
  • Asking gentle questions that invite them to say more
  • Simply being present in a comment
3

Protect Anonymity

Anonymity is what makes StillHere possible. People share things here they wouldn't share anywhere else – precisely because they don't have to attach their name to it.

Never attempt to identify another user. Do not share, guess, or speculate about who someone is. Do not screenshot or share posts outside the platform in a way that could identify the author.

This also means protecting your own anonymity. Don't share combinations of personal details that could identify you if you don't want to be identified – even on an anonymous post, a very specific set of details can be recognisable to people who know you.

A note on screenshots

Sharing content from StillHere publicly – even if it seems anonymised – can harm the person who wrote it. People share here with the expectation of a contained, safe space. Please honour that.

4

No Medical Advice

StillHere is not a medical or clinical platform. Nobody here is your therapist, psychiatrist, or doctor – even if they happen to be one in real life.

  • Do not suggest specific medications, dosages, or treatments.
  • Do not diagnose others or suggest they have a particular condition.
  • Do not discourage someone from seeking professional help.
  • Do not present personal experience with treatment as universal medical fact.

You can absolutely share your own experience – "I've been through something similar and therapy helped me" is different from "you need therapy." The first is a story. The second is a prescription.

5

Prohibited Content

The following content will be removed and may result in account suspension:

Harassment & targeting Directing hostility, mockery, or threats at any user
Hate speech Content that degrades people based on identity
Doxxing Sharing or attempting to uncover another person's identity
Spam & automation Repetitive, automated, or promotional posting
Impersonation Pretending to be another user or StillHere staff
Harmful external links Links to dangerous, illegal, or exploitative content
6

Self-Harm & Crisis Content

This is one of the most sensitive areas of these guidelines. We want StillHere to be a space where people can talk about dark feelings – including suicidal thoughts, self-harm, and hopelessness – because keeping those feelings unspoken is often more dangerous than saying them.

At the same time, there's a difference between expressing pain and content that could actively harm someone else who reads it.

Talking about struggle is allowed and encouraged. "I've been having thoughts of hurting myself" is a vulnerable, human statement. It belongs here. What doesn't belong is detailed method descriptions, content that glorifies or romanticises self-harm, or anything that could function as instructions.

Specifically prohibited

  • Detailed descriptions of self-harm methods or means
  • Content that presents self-harm or suicide as romantic, heroic, or desirable
  • Encouraging or facilitating another person's self-harm
  • Pro-eating disorder content of any kind

If you see someone in crisis

If a post suggests someone may be in immediate danger, please report it and encourage them to reach out to a crisis line. Our Crisis Hotlines page has resources for every region.

7

How to Respond Well

Responding to someone who is struggling is a skill. Here are some principles that tend to help:

01
Read the whole post before responding. People often say the most important thing at the end, after building up to it.
02
Reflect before redirecting. "That sounds really exhausting" before anything else. Let them know they've been heard.
03
Ask, don't assume. "Do you want to talk more about this?" or "Would it help to hear how I dealt with something similar?" gives them the choice.
04
Short responses can be powerful. "I see you. I'm glad you shared this." – sometimes that's exactly enough.
05
Take care of yourself too. Reading heavy content can affect you. It's okay to step back. You don't have to respond to everything.
8

Reporting Content

If you see something that violates these guidelines, please report it using the ··· menu on any post or comment. Reports are anonymous – the person you report will not know it was you.

We review all reports. We're a small team, so response times may vary – but we take every report seriously, especially anything involving safety.

Please only report genuine violations. False or bad-faith reports make it harder for us to respond quickly to real ones. Disagreeing with someone's opinion or finding a post uncomfortable is not a reason to report it.
9

Consequences

We approach moderation with proportionality. Most situations don't require a ban – they require a conversation or a warning. But some do.

Situation Likely outcome
First minor violation (e.g. advice on No Advice post)Content removed, gentle notice
Repeated minor violationsWarning, temporary posting restriction
Harassment or targeted hostilityContent removed, account warning or suspension
Doxxing or serious privacy violationImmediate suspension, possible permanent ban
Self-harm facilitation or dangerous contentImmediate removal, possible permanent ban
Repeated severe violationsPermanent ban

We reserve the right to act immediately and without warning when user safety is at risk.

10

Contact & Appeals

If your content was removed or your account was actioned and you believe it was a mistake, you can appeal.

Moderation Appeals support@stillhere.global
General Questions @still_here_global on Telegram

We aim to respond to appeals within 7 business days. Please include the post or comment in question and a brief explanation of why you believe the moderation decision was incorrect.

— crisis resources

if you need help now.

this isn't a hotline — but here's a list of free, 24/7 lines by country, plus specialized support for grief, youth, LGBTQ+, and chat-based help.

updatedmay 2026
freeall of them
24/7most of them
if you are in immediate danger

please call your local emergency number now — 112 (most of europe), 911 (us / canada), 999 (uk / hong kong), 000 (australia), 110 / 119 (japan).

if you're not in physical danger but need a trained human to talk to, the lines below are free, anonymous, and 24/7 in most countries.

grief & loss

  • cruse bereavement (uk) — 0808 808 1677.

  • compassionate friends — support after losing a child, branches worldwide.
— 09

online & chat support

if calling feels like too much:

  • imalive.org — peer chat with trained volunteers (us).
  • crisis text line — text-based, US/UK/Canada/Ireland.
  • 7 cups — free emotional support listeners, worldwide.
  • befrienders worldwide — directory of crisis lines in 32+ countries.
  • findahelpline.com — search any country, any topic.

russian-language resources:

  • pomogi.org — directory of free psychological help in Russian.
  • b17.ru — large psychologist directory; many offer a free first consultation.
  • "your territory" — free online chat & calls for teens and young adults.
  • "we're near" — online psychological support with a live chat option.
  • telemedicine — Russian Ministry of Health — official hotline & online consultations.
note — these numbers are checked carefully but can change. if a line doesn't work, please use findahelpline.com to find a current one for your country, and please tell us so we can update this list.

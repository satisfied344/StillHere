Privacy Policy

How StillHere collects, protects, and respects your data – written in plain language, because you deserve to understand it.

Effective date: April 13, 2026 Last updated: April 13, 2026 Applies to: stillhere.global
Our core promise: StillHere is a space for presence, not data collection. We only collect what's strictly necessary to run the platform. Your stories belong to you. Your identity is yours to protect.
1

Who We Are

StillHere (stillhere.global) is a community platform where people can anonymously share stories, thoughts, feelings – from the deeply personal to the wonderfully absurd. We believe in the power of being heard without judgment.

This Privacy Policy explains what personal data we process, why, and what rights you have – in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the UK GDPR, and other applicable privacy laws.

The data controller is the StillHere project team. You can reach us at the contact details in Section 12.

How We Use Your Data

We use the data collected for the following purposes, each grounded in a legal basis under GDPR:

  • Providing the service – operating the platform, displaying posts, managing accounts. (Legal basis: Contract)
  • Safety & abuse prevention – detecting spam, harassment, and content that violates our guidelines. (Legal basis: Legitimate Interest)
  • Security – protecting accounts from unauthorised access, preventing DDOS attacks. (Legal basis: Legitimate Interest)
  • Anonymous platform analytics – understanding how the platform is used in aggregate to improve it. No individual profiling. (Legal basis: Legitimate Interest)
  • Notifying you – transactional emails only: password resets, moderation notices. No marketing without explicit consent. (Legal basis: Consent / Contract)
  • Legal compliance – retaining data when required by law. (Legal basis: Legal Obligation)
We never sell your data. We never share your data with advertisers. We never build advertising profiles. StillHere is ad-free.
4

Anonymity on StillHere

StillHere was built with anonymity as a core design principle, not an afterthought. Here's how it works in practice:

Posts and comments

When you publish a post or comment, your display name is shown – which can be a pseudonym. Your real identity (email, IP) is never attached to public posts or visible to other users.

The "Anonymous" mode

You can post in fully anonymous mode. In this case, no display name or account is linked to the post at all. Even other registered users cannot tell who posted it.

Internal linkage

For moderation purposes only, we maintain an internal technical log linking IP addresses to posts for 30 days. This is used exclusively to enforce our Community Guidelines and prevent abuse. This data is never shared.

Important: No anonymity system is perfect. Please don't include information in your posts that could identify you if you don't want to be identified – such as a very specific combination of details about your life.

Crisis Resources & Psychological Support

StillHere provides links to third-party crisis hotlines and psychological support services as a directory. When you click on an external crisis resource link:

  • You leave our platform and are subject to that service's own privacy policy.
  • We do not transmit any of your data to these services automatically.
  • We do not track whether or how you use external support resources.
  • Listings are provided in good faith; we do not endorse any specific service.

For on-platform psychological support features (such as "Words of Support" and community responses), all data is governed by this policy.

Your Rights

Under GDPR and applicable laws, you have the following rights:

  • Right of access – request a copy of all data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification – correct inaccurate data.
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten") – delete your account and all associated data.
  • Right to restriction – ask us to stop processing your data in certain ways.
  • Right to data portability – receive your data in a machine-readable format.
  • Right to object – object to processing based on legitimate interest.
  • Right to withdraw consent – at any time, for any consent-based processing.
  • Right to lodge a complaint – with your national data protection authority.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at privacy@stillhere.global. We will respond within 30 days.

Security Measures

We take security seriously, especially given the sensitive nature of the content shared on StillHere. Our measures include:

  • TLS encryption in transit for all connections.
  • Passwords hashed using bcrypt with a per-user salt – we never store plain text passwords.
  • Database encryption at rest.
  • Access controls – only necessary team members can access production data.
  • Regular security audits and dependency updates.
  • Rate limiting and abuse detection to protect all users.
Despite our efforts, no system is 100% secure. In the event of a data breach affecting your personal data, we will notify you within 72 hours as required by GDPR.

Children's Privacy

StillHere is not directed at children under the age of 16. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe a child under 16 has registered, please contact us and we will delete their account promptly.

Given the sensitive nature of some content on the platform, we recommend that users under 18 use the platform with parental awareness.
